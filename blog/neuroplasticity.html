Neuroplasticity Research

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

The principles of neuroplasticity benefit people by aiding them in learning new skills.

For example, right now I am trying to utilise neuroplasticity while learning to code by making sure I practice my coding abilities for at least 2 hours everyday (I expect this to increase as the course goes on) even when I am doing these cultural research blogs.
This should significantly help my learning journey by maintaining skills and learning new skills.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.

I will engage with the principles of neuroplasticity by coding for at least 2 hours per day during foundations. I expect this to increase in bootcamp.
I will also take care of my body, going for at least a 15 minute walk everyday and working out when I can.
I am also trying to maintain a good sleep schedule throughout the course in order to properly rest my body.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

I would recommend to you to follow a similar type of daily lifestyle. I find it greatly helps me learn new abilities.
Try your best to look after your body and mind even when life gets busy and/or stressful.

Growth Mindset Research

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

Growth mindset is a different way of viewing obstacles and decisions. It is knowing that you can improve at anything with work. It is relevant because in order to improve we need to reflect and acknowledge failures, and find reasons to keep growing the mind (growth mindset).

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

I found it surprising that I might’ve been in a ‘fixed’ mindset in some aspects when it came to my own learning. A fixed mindset is basically the opposite to the growth mindset.
Thinking that all growth is a static, non-malleable thing and that you are either good at something or you are bad at something. I am going to change my mindset to knowing that I can improve at something, even if I am not great at it straight away.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

I will integrate a growth mindset by reflecting on things that go wrong and trying to improve those things for next time. This is a fairly large shift in my mindset so I may need some time to work on this, but I can change it.

For starters I will implement a growth mindset into every piece of new knowledge. I believe this will greatly improve the speed at which I gain new skills, simply by not being too hard on myself in my education and knowing that I can make mistakes.
