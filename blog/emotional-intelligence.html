Emotional Intelligence

EQ and IQ

This is a quick post on my research into Emotional Intelligence(EQ) and Intelligence Quotient (IQ)

We will cover three main topics in this post

  • What is Emotional Intelligence?
  • How is EQ different to IQ?
  • Why is EQ important?

1. Mental health America defines Emotional Intelligence as “the ability to manage both your own emotions and understand the emotions of people around you”. In other words, it is a “measure (no score though)” for your ability to perceive others emotions and understand their perspective as well as your ability to use emotions to navigate social situations.

2. EQ is different from IQ in that IQ is a scoring system to measure one’s processing, memory and critical thinking abilities. While EQ is a rough measure of emotional competency. It involves looking at emotions to identify a social situation. It affects empathy, relationships, self awareness, self control and motivation. There are multiple types of EQ. There is a set ‘score’ for IQ that you can find from a standardised test while EQ is a bit harder to measure, the score is not from a test so to speak, but rather from experiencing social situations.

3. I am going to answer this question from the perspective of the workplace, but I truly believe EQ is deeply important in all aspects of life. It is important for people in charge to view things objectively by keeping a level head and taking a step back when confrontations or tensions arise in a team. Understanding and empathy are deeply important for conflict solving, whether it be in a tension scenario or in a non-verbal situation. Recognising there may be problems without someone speaking and at least communicating about it makes a huge difference in teamwork.
