Foundations Reflections

Answering Some Questions about Human Skills and Foundations

  • What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

  1. I learned that I get a lot out of listening and looping, it really helps me to just talk about what I am doing and struggling with with another person
  2. I’ve learned to take constructive criticism better
  3. My mindset has entered a ‘when can i learn this’ rather than a ‘im not smart enough for this’ mindset.

  • What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

All of these are important to programming.

Values are something to learn from other people (not just in your programming team, but in life in general) as well as upholding and being proud of your own.

Empathy is very, very important while working in a team as you have to take time to understand and care about others while working on creative projects together.

Otherwise people may feel uncomfortable and/or unmotivated and that may take a toll on their work. Self-awareness is knowing when to ask for help and what to ask for.

Knowing something is going wrong(or right) for you means that you can focus on changing or maintaining that thing.

Being comfortable in a learning or programming environment means you can learn or work productively and safely.

  • What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

Shifting my own mindset was challenging. I did a lot of retrospective thinking to understand my own mindset wasn’t helping me learn and had to change it.

Also getting into a habit of practicing mindfulness by doing daily meditations was its own challenge.

  • Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

DevAcademy,in my opinion, spends time focusing on human skills to prepare its students for working in the real world.

The idea is that these human skills are getting you ready to work productively in a team and know your own limitations and/or boundaries when working in a productive environment/in creative projects.

  • Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

No. Human skills does not feel like a waste of time.

DevAcademy is trying to get you a job after you graduate and, excuse my French, no one wants to work with a dick.

Human Skills help you adjust to working in a team and working with a bad teammate isn’t a good time.
